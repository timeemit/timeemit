I’ve performed all this work as a software engineer yet I hold no formal degree in computer science: my education is in mathematics and physics. My engineering skillset is the result of a dedicated transition from academic physics through constant study of online resources and a passionate love for building out new ideas. Four consecutive summer internships working in research labs sparked my interest in programming as I often wrote software to automate repetitive calculations and analysis of lab results. I’ve since accrued my engineering prowess through an insatiable ingestion of resources that either fed my curiosity of software design or fueled my capacity to build a never-ending list of personal projects. When it comes to software, I take the phrase “learning-by-doing” very seriously.
